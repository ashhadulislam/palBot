Since the establishment of Israel in 1948, deportation has been used to expel Palestinians from their land, both individually and en masse. The Fourth Geneva Convention, adopted in 1949, prohibits mass deportation from occupied territories.

 

Deportation or forced displacement of people from occupied territories is also seen as a war crime under the Rome Statue of the International Criminal Court.

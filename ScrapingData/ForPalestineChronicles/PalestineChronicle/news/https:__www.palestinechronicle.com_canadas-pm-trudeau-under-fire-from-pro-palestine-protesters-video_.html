Canadian PM Justin Trudeau interrupted by protesters inside a Vancouver restaurant. (Photo: video grab) Share Tweet Pin Email Share Around 100 police officers were deployed to disperse a crowd of pro-Palestine protesters who surrounded a restaurant where Canadian Prime Minister Justin Trudeau was dining in Vancouver on Tuesday night. The protesters, numbering about 250, chanted for a ceasefire in the ongoing onslaught on the Gaza Strip. Two people were reportedly arrested. Videos showed protesters waving Palestinian flags, shouting slogans and jeering Trudeau outside the restaurant. Earlier on Tuesday, Trudeau was heckled by protesters inside another restaurant in a different part of the city. A video posted to social media shows activists confronting the Prime Minister while he was seated at a corner table. Charlotte Kates, an organizer with the Samidoun Palestinian Prisoner Solidarity Network who attended both protests, reportedly said that activists demanded that Canada “take a real position” on Israel’s military assault in Gaza. Protests in the city, as in other cities across Canada, have been ongoing since Israel’s latest operation began. Also on Tuesday, Trudeau called on Israel to stop “this killing of women, of children, of babies” in the Gaza Strip. “This has to stop,” he told a news conference. “I urge the government of Israel to exercise maximum restraint. The world is watching, on TV, on social media – we’re hearing the testimonies of doctors, family members, survivors, kids who have lost their parents,” he said.

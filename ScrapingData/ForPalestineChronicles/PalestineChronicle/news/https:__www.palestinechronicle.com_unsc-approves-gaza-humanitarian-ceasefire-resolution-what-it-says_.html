The United Nations Security Council passed a resolution on Wednesday calling for urgent and extended humanitarian corridors throughout the besieged Gaza Strip to save and protect civilian lives. This came following four unsuccessful attempts to take action regarding the matter last month Today’s resolution was drafted by Malta. The United States, Russia, and Britain, which are council veto powers, abstained from the vote. Russia failed in its bid to amend the resolution and call for an immediate humanitarian truce leading to a permanent cessation of hostilities. The United States and Britain abstained because the resolution failed to condemn the Palestinian Resistance Movement Hamas. It also calls for the “immediate and unconditional release of all hostages (…), especially children”. The text also calls on, “all parties to refrain from depriving the civilian population in the Gaza Strip of basic services and humanitarian assistance indispensable to their survival, consistent with international humanitarian law, which has a disproportionate impact on children”. Following the vote, Chinese UN Ambassador Zhang Jun said that the Council should have adopted a more robust resolution earlier. “The key rests on the implementation of the resolution and its provisions to the letter”, he said.
